I am an AI engineer, not an AI security expert. This distinction matters.

My background helps me understand how models are built and integrated. It does not automatically give me the adversarial mindset or practical security experience required to assess those systems well. I am building those skills deliberately—and documenting the process here.

## Why security

Modern AI applications retrieve private data, call tools, retain memory, write code, and sometimes act with a user's authority. Their failures extend beyond an incorrect answer.

> How do we build useful AI systems without giving uncertain model behavior unchecked authority?

## How I will document progress

For each subject, I intend to publish:

1. Notes explaining the concept in my own words.
2. A small experiment or reproducible test.
3. What failed or remained confusing.
4. The next question the work produced.

Some early notes will be incomplete. Lab results may be negative. My understanding will change. That is the point of keeping a public notebook rather than presenting a polished expert persona.

I want claims on this site to lead to evidence: code, diagrams, experiment logs, references, or an explicit status such as *planned* or *in progress*. Progress should be visible without pretending the destination has already been reached.
